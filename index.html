I have served as a Part-time Lab Officer at North South University and currently looking for opportunities to pursue higher study.

I completed my Bachelor’s in Computer Science and Engineering at North South University. I, along with a team of 3 members designed and executed an underwater research and rescue robot as our final year capstone project, which was supervised by Dr. Mohammad Monirujjaman Khan. I was a Teaching Assistant in my department and completed my Internship at The Tech Academy, where I worked on developing and introducing new content for course materials over electronics and programming courses. I like to travel a lot and try new foods. I watch TV series during my free time and play games, though I am not good at it.

Research

From the beginning of my student life, I had an interest in automation. During my undergrad, I took 3 courses from the Artificial Intelligence Trail (Artificial Intelligence, Image Processing, and Neural Network) and got very much interested in this field. After graduation, I wanted to explore more and started working on different fields of machine learning, e.g, Neural Networks, Convolutional Neural Networks, Deep Neural Networks, etc. where I worked to establish classification systems like violent detection, child labor detection, sexual harassment detection, etc.

Sexual Harassment Detection

The 6th IEEE international Women in Engineering (WIE) Conference on Electrical and Computer Engineering 2020 (IEEE WIECON-ECE 2020)

Proposed a novel dataset along with a model to detect sexual harassment. (Under Review)

Authors : MD. Tanzil Shahria, Farhat Tasnim Progga, Saif Ahmed

A Deep Learning Based Approach to Child Labour Detection

The Information Technology International Seminar (ITIS)

Developed a Convolutional Neural Network-based model to classify child labor from usual normal working people. As such dataset is not very common, we proposed a unique dataset of child labor as well in the context of South Asian region. (In press)

Authors : Farhat Tasnim Progga, MD. Tanzil Shahria, Asma Arisha, Minhaz Uddin Ahmed Shanto

Collector: A Vision-based Semi-autonomous Robot for Mangrove Forest Exploration and Research

The 2019 International Conference on Mechatronics, Robotics and Systems Engineering (MoRSE)

A vision semi-autonomous surveillance robot was designed that can traverse in remote and difficult terrain within the muddy surface and collect, store and send data from different sensors to base station and its server.

Authors : MD. Tanzil Shahria, Aimon Rahman, Hasib Zunair, Shoaib Bin Aziz.

Development of a Curriculum to Teach Electronics to Workers of Garments Industry in Bangladesh: A Visual Literacy Approach

The 2019 IEEE International Conference on Engineering, Technology and Education (TALE)

A part of an ongoing series of work to effectively introduce vocational education in electrical systems and electronics to female garments workers of Bangladesh has been presented in this paper.

Authors : MD. Tanzil Shahria, Soumic Shekhar, Muhammad Irfanul Haque, Lamia Iftekhar

Collaborative Group Learning in Programming Classes

The 2019 IEEE International Conference on Engineering, Technology and Education (TALE)

An innovative and collaborative way to engage students in a programming class has been presented in this study.

Authors : MD. Tanzil Shahria, Nova Ahmed.

Violent Crowd Flow Detection Using Deep Learning

Asian Conference on Intelligent Information and Database Systems

Convolutional neural networks (CNN) and long short-term memory network (LSTM) based architectures have been experimented separately to detect violent crowd flows in the context of Bangladesh. We have collected a dataset as well which includes both violent and non-violent crowd flows.

Authors : Shakil Ahmed Sumon, MD. Tanzil Shahria, MD. Raihan Goni, Nazmul Hasan, A. M. Almarufuzzaman, Rashedur M. Rahman.

Underwater Research and Rescue Robot

The 2019 Third IEEE International Conference on Electrical, Computer and Communication Technologies

An underwater robot was designed, implemented and tested, which can go under the water and send live video feed without any delay.

Authors : MD. Tanzil Shahria, Kimia Tuz Zaman, Sabrina Rabbi, Mohammad Monirujjaman Khan.
