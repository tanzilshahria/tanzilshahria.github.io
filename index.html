Md Tanzil Shahria

I am a Ph.D. student, working on Biorobotics under the supervision of Dr. Mohammad Habibur Rahman at the University of Wisconsin-Milwaukee. I'm also a Teaching Assistant at the University of Wisconsin-Milwaukee's Computer Science Department. I formerly worked at Advanced Software Technology International Limited in Bangladesh as the Chief Business Development Officer. I've also worked as a part-time Lab Officer at North South University and a part-time Lecturer at Presidency University, Bangladesh.

I completed my Bachelor’s in Computer Science and Engineering at North South University. As our final year capstone project, Dr. Mohammad Monirujjaman Khan supervised me and our team of three members to build an underwater research and rescue robot. I worked as a Teaching Assistant in my department and finished an internship at The Tech Academy, where I developed and introduced new content for course materials in electronics and programming. As a research assistant, I worked under Dr. Lamia Iftekhar on a funded project named "Area Coverage Algorithms for Networked Multi-robot Systems," where my role was to construct the hardware model. During my undergraduate years, I was also associated with IEEE, where I was elected for consecutive two years as the Chair of the IEEE North South University Student Branch, Robotics and Automation Society, under the direction of Dr. Lamia Iftekhar.

I enjoy traveling and trying different foods. In my spare time, I enjoy watching TV shows and playing games, though I am not good at it.

Research

Automation, computer vision, and vision-based control have always captured my interest since I was a freshman in college. I wanted to learn more, so I began working on Neural Networks, Convolutional Neural Networks, Deep Neural Networks, and other fields of machine learning, where I developed classification systems for things like violent detection, child labor detection, and sexual harassment detection, among other things. After that, I was accepted as a Ph.D. student in Computer Science at the University of Wisconsin-Milwaukee and was given the opportunity to work at the Biorobotics Lab at UWM, where I am focusing on vision-based robot manipulation. In the lab, I'm also collaborating on mixed reality projects.
